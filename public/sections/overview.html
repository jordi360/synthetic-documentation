Synthetic Data for Computer Vision

Computer vision models require thousands of correctly annotated images for training. However, the need to gather that data in a short period of time hasn’t been solved when it comes to real-life images. Collecting real-world visual data with desired characteristics and diversity can be prohibitively expensive and time-consuming. This is where the concept of synthetic data generation comes into play.

Synthetic images are generated programmatically, eliminating the need for manual data collection efforts. This approach allows for the creation of diverse datasets with nearly perfect annotations.

In this documentation, we present AIS REV 360’s synthetic environment, a comprehensive system designed to facilitate the generation of realistic synthetic images tailored for computer vision tasks. Through the integration of Blender for 3D modeling and rendering, combined with Python for scripting and automation, our project provides an efficient solution for producing the necessary training data to accelerate the development of robust models.
